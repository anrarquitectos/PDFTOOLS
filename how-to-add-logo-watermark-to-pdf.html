How to add your logo as a watermark to all your plans, without software

Sending a full drawing set to a client or contractor and want your studio logo on every sheet. This tutorial shows how to stamp it across all your PDFs at once, with the right opacity and position, entirely in your browser.

As an architect I regularly needed to send out drawing sets with a discreet studio logo on every sheet, as both branding and a basic layer of protection against misuse. Doing it sheet by sheet in Acrobat is slow, and most free tools either rasterize the result or handle multi-file batches poorly. The logo watermark tool processes any number of PDFs at once and outputs a ZIP. You can find it here.

The logo is automatically converted to grayscale before embedding, which keeps it neutral against any drawing background and avoids color conflicts with drawing content.

STEP Load your PDFs and your logo image

Open the Logo watermark tool and click Choose PDF(s). You can select an entire drawing set at once — ten, fifty or more files. Once loaded, the button confirms how many PDFs are ready.

Then click Choose image and select your logo file. PNG with a transparent background works best. JPG is also accepted. The tool will convert it to grayscale automatically, so the original colors of your logo file do not matter.

A few practical notes

PNG logos with a transparent background produce the cleanest result. If your logo has a white background, it will appear as a white rectangle on the drawing. In that case, either use a PNG with transparency, or increase the rotation so the rectangle shape is less noticeable at low opacity.

The grayscale conversion uses luminance weighting, so a logo with strong contrast in its original colors will still read clearly after conversion. A logo that is mostly mid-gray at the same luminance level as the drawing background may become harder to read — in that case, try a slightly higher opacity or a corner position.
